Gigwallet ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we store and use it, and your rights as a user of the Gigwallet iOS application.

01Collection

Data We Collect

We collect only the information necessary to deliver the features of Gigwallet:

Identity

Full name and email address collected during account registration.

Location

Approximate or precise location, used to surface local gig opportunities and earnings data.

Financial Data

Income records, linked bank account metadata, and transaction data facilitated through Plaid.

Usage Data

App interactions and feature usage patterns collected to improve performance and reliability.

We do not collect payment card numbers directly. Financial connections are handled exclusively through Plaid's encrypted infrastructure.

02Storage

How Your Data Is Stored

Gigwallet uses a layered storage approach designed with privacy in mind:

  • On-Device (SwiftData): Your financial records, income logs, and personal settings are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly sync or share it.
  • Firebase Authentication: Your authentication credentials are securely stored and managed by Google Firebase, which employs industry-standard encryption at rest and in transit.
  • Plaid: Bank connection tokens and account metadata are stored within Plaid's secure infrastructure, governed by their own privacy and security standards.
Your most sensitive financial data lives on your device. We architected Gigwallet this way deliberately — your earnings history is yours, not ours.
03Third Parties

Third-Party Services

Gigwallet integrates with the following external services. Each operates under its own terms and privacy policy:

  • Firebase Authentication (Google): Used for secure account creation, login, and session management. Firebase Privacy Policy →
  • Plaid: Used to securely connect and read data from your bank accounts. Plaid Privacy Policy →
  • Ticketmaster API: Used to surface local event-based gig opportunities. No personal data is transmitted beyond what is necessary for the location search.
  • EIA (U.S. Energy Information Administration) API: Used to provide fuel price context for gig drivers. This is a public government API; no personal data is transmitted.
  • FRED API (Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis): Used for economic data and income benchmarking. This is a public API; no personal data is transmitted.
04Usage

How We Use Your Data

We use the data we collect exclusively to operate and improve Gigwallet:

  • Authenticate and maintain your account
  • Display personalized earnings summaries and financial insights
  • Surface relevant gig opportunities in your area
  • Generate income estimates and expense breakdowns
  • Diagnose technical issues and improve app stability

We do not use your data for targeted advertising, sell your data to data brokers, or share your data with any third party for their own marketing purposes.

05Sharing

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We share necessary data with Firebase and Plaid solely to provide app functionality.
  • Legal obligations: We may disclose information if required by law or valid legal process (e.g., a court order or subpoena).
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger or acquisition, your data may be transferred. We will notify you before such a transfer occurs.
06Deletion

Data Retention & Deletion

You may delete your Gigwallet account at any time directly within the app:

  1. Open Gigwallet and navigate to Settings
  2. Select Account
  3. Tap Delete Account and confirm

Upon deletion, your Firebase account is permanently removed within 30 days. On-device SwiftData is deleted immediately when the app is uninstalled. Plaid connection tokens are revoked automatically upon account deletion. We retain anonymized analytics data for up to 12 months.

07CCPA

California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act:

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of the categories and pieces of personal information we've collected about you in the past 12 months.
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Opt-Out: We do not sell personal information. You may contact us to confirm this at any time.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any CCPA right.
To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@gigwallet.app with the subject line "CCPA Request." We will respond within 45 days as required by law.
08Children

Children's Privacy

Gigwallet is not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a minor, please contact us immediately at privacy@gigwallet.app and we will take steps to delete it promptly.
